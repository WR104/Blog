Evolution vs. Intelligent Design Debate

“Where do we come from?”

This question is like the constant and permanent sky, no one has been able to give a clear answer. Why does life exist in our world and where did it first come from? Different cultures have different answers to this question. There are two main explanations about the origin of life, one is called intelligent design and the other is called evolutionism. In fact, there are problems with both creationism and evolution that they cannot explain clearly, and we cannot prove that either theory is absolutely correct. Both creationism and evolution have their own advocates, but it is obvious that they cannot be correct at the same time. So it is necessary to examine the reasons for each of the two theories, and the problems with them.

why is intelligent design/creationism still popular in America?

Intelligent Design is a broader theory of creationism, emphasizing only that life must be created by the design of an intelligent Creator, and cannot be created through purely natural processes. Instead of limiting the attributes of God and the specific time and order of the birth of everything, Intelligent Design no longer adheres to the text of Genesis or even relies on the Bible, but takes logical reasoning and scientific data as the basis for the existence of Intelligent Design. There are three primary arguments of intelligent design, namely, “irreducible complexity”, “specific complexity”, and “Fine-tuned universe”.[1] The basic idea can be summarized in one sentence: complex systems must have a designer. Although the advocates of intelligent design have mostly avoided the identity of the “designer” and carefully avoided the word “God”. However, to many, it still seems to be entangled with creationism, or even a replica of it. Most advocates of intelligent design only emphasize the need for an intelligent creator to intervene in the creation of life, regardless of whether the creator himself is God; whether the creator has the attributes of omniscience, omnibenevolence, and omnipotence; and the exact timing and sequence of his creation. At the same time, Intelligent Designers do not deny the evolution of living things, but they believe that Darwinian evolution can only explain minor evolution, but not the major evolution that produces new species. Evolution is not only a purely natural phenomenon based on random mutation and natural selection, but there must be an element of intelligent design in it, or evolution itself is a means by which the Creator created life.

Point of Evolutionary theroy

Evolutionary theory holds that life was not created by God, or some other supernatural being. Rather, it evolved gradually from inanimate matter over a long period of time in nature. Of course, this evolutionary process is very long, and could not be completed in six days as God created all things. At present, the scientific community generally believes that the evolution of life on earth has been about 3.5 billion years long, and the process of evolution has not stopped until now. And as long as life still exists, evolution will never stop. Evolutionary theory believes that the emergence of higher life like humans is the result of numerous chance events, and there is no inevitability in it. If it were not for the accidental growth of bones in the fins of some ancient fish, and their risky decision to land, there might not have been vertebrates on land at all; If it wasn’t for a meteorite impact that wiped out the entire dinosaur species, tiny mammals might never have had a chance to dominate the planet; If the climate of the African savannah hadn’t become arid, forcing the southern apes to abandon jungle life and walk upright, there might not have been humans at all.

The debate

The main difference between evolutionary theory and intelligent design theory is that thoroughgoing evolutionary theory stands entirely on a physicalist position to explain the phenomena of life. It holds that all living phenomena arise through purely natural evolutionary processes and that there is no creator of the universe. Intelligent design theory, on the other hand, believes that something as complex as life cannot be produced through a purely natural process, and that the creation of life must depend on an intelligent designer, or must be controlled by the participation of God. The main counterpoint to intelligent design is to point out the problem of so-called “bad design”. That is, it states that the structure of an organism is far from perfect, and that there are many irrationalities in it. This imperfect structure must not come from the design of an intelligent designer, but can only come through natural evolution. In fact, as early as more than 200 years ago, David Hume put forward almost the same point of view. There is such a passage in “_Dialogues Concerning Natural Religion_”: “This world, for aught he knows, is very faulty and imperfect, compared to a superior standard; and was only the first rude essay of some infant deity, who afterwards abandoned it, ashamed of his lame performance: it is the work only of some dependent, inferior deity; and is the object of derision to his superiors: it is the production of old age and dotage in some superannuated deity; and ever since his death, has run on at adventures, from the first impulse and active force which it received from him.” Modern evolutionary theory’s refutation of the intelligent design theory is based on the same idea which is divided into two main areas as follows: The presence of useless organs in the body of animals can only be explained as vestiges of evolution, and cannot be the deliberate design of an intelligent creator; The irrationality that exists in the body structure of certain animals points out that this must not be the design of an intelligent God, but rather a choice of last resort when evolving into the organs of a new species based on the organs of their ancestors.[2] On the other hand, although intelligent design has made many improvements to creation theory, it still cannot completely solve the problems left by creation theory. For example, how to explain the problem of such a large number of species. It is difficult to answer the question of why there are so many species of organisms in the world if only microevolution within species is recognized, without recognizing the grand evolution that can produce new species.

While understanding evolution and intelligent design, it should be noted that those modern theories are only theoretical extrapolations based on observation and experimentation. They are not irreversible truths. Human beings need to make unremitting efforts in order to answer the ultimate question of “where do we come from”.

[1]: Cosmic Creationism: Ken Wilber’s Theory of Evolution

[2]: Coyne, J. A. (2010). Why evolution is true. Viking.
